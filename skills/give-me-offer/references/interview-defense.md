# Interview Defense

Use this file after rewriting strong bullets, especially when the resume has been tailored aggressively for a role.

## Purpose

A resume bullet is only safe if the candidate can defend it in an interview. Strong wording without prepared explanation creates rejection risk.

## Defense Checklist

For each strong bullet, produce:

```text
Resume bullet:
Evidence:
Likely follow-up questions:
Safe answer outline:
Risk level: low | medium | high
Wording downgrade if needed:
```

## Common Follow-Up Questions

### Ownership

- Which part did you personally do?
- Was this independent or team work?
- How many people were involved?
- Who reviewed or accepted the work?

### Technical Depth

- Why did you choose this tool or method?
- What was the hardest problem?
- How did you debug or test it?
- What would you improve next?

### Metrics

- Where did this number come from?
- How was it measured?
- Was it your direct contribution?
- Is there proof?

### Career Switch

- Why are you switching?
- Why this role instead of your major/current role?
- What have you already done to prepare?
- What gap are you still closing?

### AI-Related Claims

- Did you build this yourself or use a tool?
- What model/API/framework did you use?
- How did you evaluate outputs?
- What are the limitations?

## Risk Levels

### Low

The candidate has evidence and can explain details.

Action: keep strong wording.

### Medium

The claim is true but details are incomplete or contribution is shared.

Action: use conservative ownership language and prepare explanation.

### High

The claim is vague, unsupported, or beyond the candidate's actual ability.

Action: downgrade or remove.

## Downgrade Patterns

Use these when a bullet is too strong:

- "Led" -> "Participated in" or "Supported"
- "Owned" -> "Implemented the [specific part]"
- "Optimized by X%" -> "Improved [process] through [method]" unless X is verified
- "Expert in" -> "Hands-on experience with"
- "Built an AI Agent platform" -> "Built a prototype AI workflow" if it was a demo

## Final Interview Prep Output

```text
Top 5 bullets to defend:
Questions likely to be asked:
Answers to prepare:
Claims to downgrade:
Proof materials to bring:
```
