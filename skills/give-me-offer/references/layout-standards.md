# Layout Standards

Use this file when creating or auditing resume layout, especially for China-facing campus recruitment, graduate recruitment, early-career switching, and ordinary social recruitment.

## Default Rule: One Page First

Default to one A4 page for:

- undergraduate and graduate campus recruitment
- entry-level candidates
- candidates with less than about 5 years of relevant experience
- career switchers whose target-role evidence must be selective
- internet, product, operations, business, finance, HR, education, and most standard corporate applications

Allow two pages only when the candidate has enough relevant content:

- senior or specialist candidates with 6+ years of directly relevant experience
- research CVs with publications, patents, grants, or academic projects
- technical leaders with substantial project depth
- state-owned enterprise, public institution, or formal application systems that request detailed records

If using two pages, page 1 must still contain the full value proposition: target role, education/work summary, core skills, and strongest experiences.

## Domestic Company Friendly Layout

Prefer a clean, restrained, information-dense A4 style:

- single column
- black or dark gray text
- one accent color at most, used sparingly for section headings or thin lines
- clear section hierarchy
- enough whitespace to breathe, but no decorative emptiness
- no large portrait unless the market specifically expects a photo
- no heavy icons, charts, skill bars, progress bars, QR-heavy design, or infographic blocks

The resume should look like a serious business document, not a poster.

## Recommended Structure

For China-facing campus or early-career resumes:

```text
Name + Contact + Portfolio/GitHub
Target Role
Education
Skills
Internship / Work Experience
Projects / Research
Campus / Leadership / Activities
Awards / Certificates
```

If the candidate has no internship, put projects before campus activities. If the candidate has strong work history, put work experience before education.

## Typography and Spacing

For A4 resumes:

- Chinese body: 10.5-11 pt equivalent
- English body: 10-11 pt
- Name: 18-24 pt
- Section heading: 12-14 pt
- Margins: about 12-18 mm
- Bullet spacing: compact but readable
- Line height: about 1.15-1.3
- Use consistent punctuation and date format

Avoid tiny text below 9 pt. If the resume cannot fit, cut weak content before shrinking fonts.

## ATS and Parser Friendliness

Keep key content easy for both humans and systems to parse:

- avoid tables for core resume content when possible
- avoid text boxes, headers/footers for contact details, layered shapes, and image-only text
- avoid multi-column layouts for ATS-heavy application channels
- export to PDF for visual delivery, but keep a plain Markdown/DOCX/text source if the platform parses content
- keep section names conventional
- place dates and organization names consistently

## Photo Placement

For China-facing resumes, a small professional photo may be acceptable or expected in some campus, state-owned, education, service, or traditional industries.

For international, US-style, or many foreign-company applications, do not include a photo unless requested.

If using a photo:

- keep it small and aligned near the header
- use a clean professional headshot
- avoid over-retouched or fashion-poster styles
- do not let the photo compress core content

## Content Density Rules

One-page resumes must prioritize:

1. target-role keywords and evidence
2. strongest projects and internships
3. measurable or demonstrable outcomes
4. skills that appear in the JD
5. education and credentials

Cut or compress:

- generic self-evaluation
- weak hobbies
- unrelated coursework
- repeated project descriptions
- overlong responsibility lists
- inflated soft-skill slogans

## One-Page Compression Strategy

When content overflows:

1. Remove self-evaluation if it repeats the resume.
2. Merge weak campus activities into one compact section.
3. Keep only 2-3 strongest bullets for each experience.
4. Move older or weakly relevant items out.
5. Convert tools into a compact skills line.
6. Shorten bullets before shrinking font.

## Visual Quality Checklist

Before final delivery:

- Can a recruiter identify target role, education, skills, and strongest evidence in 10 seconds?
- Does the first half of page 1 contain the most relevant proof?
- Are section headings easy to scan?
- Are bullets aligned and similarly sized?
- Is there enough whitespace between sections?
- Does the resume still work if copied as plain text?
- Are any decorative elements reducing parsability?
